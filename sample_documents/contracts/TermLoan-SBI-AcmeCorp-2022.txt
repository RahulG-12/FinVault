SECTION 4: EVENTS OF DEFAULT
================================================================================
4.1 Payment Default: Failure to pay any amount within 5 business days of due date.
4.2 Covenant Breach: Failure to maintain financial covenants (uncured within 45 days).
4.3 Cross-Default: Default under any other loan/financial instrument >INR 5 Crore.
4.4 Material Adverse Change: Any event materially adversely affecting Borrower's
    ability to repay (including credit rating downgrade below BB).
4.5 Change in Control: Any change in majority ownership without prior approval.
4.6 Misrepresentation: Any material misrepresentation in financial disclosures.
4.7 Insolvency: Filing under IBC 2016 or appointment of liquidator/receiver.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: CONSEQUENCES OF DEFAULT
================================================================================
5.1 Upon Event of Default, Lender may at its discretion:
    a) Declare the entire outstanding loan immediately due and payable
    b) Invoke personal guarantee of MD
    c) Enforce charge/mortgage on security assets
    d) Initiate SARFAESI proceedings (Securitisation Act 2002)
    e) File before Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT) or approach NCLT under IBC

5.2 Penal Interest: Additional 2% p.a. on defaulted amounts.

5.3 Right of Set-Off: Lender may set off any amounts in Borrower's accounts
    maintained with SBI against outstanding loan dues.
