1. AUDIT MANDATE & SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This internal audit was conducted pursuant to the Board Resolution dated
March 20, 2023 and covers all business operations of GlobalTech Solutions Pvt.
Ltd. including revenue recognition, accounts payable/receivable, treasury
operations, payroll, fixed assets, and IT general controls.

Audit Standards Applied: Standards on Internal Audit (SIA) issued by ICAI;
COSO Internal Control — Integrated Framework (2013 Edition).

2. EXECUTIVE AUDIT OPINION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OVERALL RATING: SATISFACTORY WITH SIGNIFICANT OBSERVATIONS

The internal control environment at GlobalTech Solutions is generally adequate
with some material weaknesses identified in accounts receivable management,
vendor due diligence, and IT access controls. Immediate remediation is required
for three HIGH severity findings detailed in Section 5.

>>> FINDING AR-01 (HIGH SEVERITY): UNDER-PROVISIONING <<<
Recommendation: Increase bad debt provision by INR 5.6 Crore to adequately

5. HIGH SEVERITY AUDIT FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FINDING H-01: ACCOUNTS RECEIVABLE PROVISIONING (as noted above)
Risk: Profit overstatement by ~INR 5.6 Crore; regulatory scrutiny risk.
Management Response Due: June 30, 2024

FINDING H-02: VENDOR PAYMENT CONTROLS
Issue: 14 vendor payments totaling INR 3.84 Crore were processed without
complete three-way matching (PO → GRN → Invoice). Of these, 3 payments
(INR 82 lakh) were made to vendors not in the approved master list.
Risk: Fraud risk, potential fictitious vendor payments.
Recommended Action: Implement mandatory system-enforced three-way match;
conduct forensic review of the 3 suspicious payments.
Management Response Due: May 31, 2024

FINDING H-03: PRIVILEGED ACCESS MANAGEMENT (IT CONTROLS)
Issue: 23 ex-employees retain active system access in ERP (SAP S/4HANA).
4 of these accounts logged activity after employment cessation dates.
Risk: Data breach, unauthorized transactions, regulatory violation (IT Act 2000).
Recommended Action: Immediate deactivation of all 23 accounts; forensic log
review for the 4 active post-separation accounts; implement automated
joiner-mover-leaver (JML) process.
Management Response Due: Immediate (within 5 business days)
