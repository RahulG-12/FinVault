EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Acme Corporation India delivered a mixed Q3 performance amid challenging
macroeconomic conditions. Revenue grew 8.3% YoY but fell 4.1% sequentially.
EBITDA margin compressed by 210 basis points due to rising input costs and
increased debt servicing obligations. The company's leverage ratio has reached
concerning levels, requiring immediate strategic attention.

>>> RISK ASSESSMENT: HIGH LEVERAGE RISK <<<
The company's Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of 4.26x has breached the covenant
threshold of 4.0x stipulated in the INR 450 Crore term loan agreement with
SBI Capital Markets (Agreement Ref: SBI/TL/ACME/2022/0034). Management has
been notified and is in active discussions with the lender for a covenant waiver.
Failure to obtain a waiver by March 31, 2025 could trigger a cross-default
clause affecting an additional INR 320 Crore in bilateral credit facilities.

RISK FACTORS & MANAGEMENT COMMENTARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. INTEREST RATE RISK: 68% of debt is floating rate. RBI's hawkish stance
   implies potential additional interest cost of INR 18-22 Crore in FY26
   if rates increase another 50 bps.

2. COVENANT BREACH RISK: As noted above, Net Debt/EBITDA at 4.26x vs
   covenant of 4.0x. Management is pursuing: (a) asset divestiture of non-core
   real estate assets worth ~INR 85 Crore, (b) equity raise of INR 120 Crore
   via QIP in Q4 FY25, and (c) renegotiation of debt covenants.

3. WORKING CAPITAL STRESS: Debtor days increased to 67 days (from 48 days in
   Q3 FY24). Creditor days decreased to 38 days. Net working capital cycle
   stretched by 25 days — tying up approximately INR 95 Crore in additional
   working capital.

4. FX RISK: Export revenues of INR 61.8 Crore exposed to USD/INR volatility.
   Current hedge ratio: 45% (below company policy of 70%).
